Description

Quiet, lightweight Honda Portable Generators deliver clean, reliable power for camping, tailgating or a home power outage.
Exclusive Honda inverter technology produces steady power to run electronics and appliances and to charge automotive-type batteries. All feature fuel-efficient 4-stroke air-cooled overhead valve engines, recoil auto start and spark arrester/muffler.

  • 2000 watts AC; 96 watts DC
  • Power for computers and most kitchen appliances
  • Runs 4 hours at 1600 watts rated load
  • Connect two for 4000 watts of portable power
  • Sound level: 59 dB at rated load

    Mfg Part #: 659820

    Tech Notes: At only 46.3 lbs, the EU2000i utilizes Honda's "state of the art" inverter technology to provide 2000 watts of lightweight, quiet and efficient power. This generator will run up to 15 hours on a single tank of gas when used with the Eco-Throttle feature. This easy starting generator produces only 45.9dB of sound at 7 meters with a full load, which is less than the rating of common speech. The Honda Inverter Technology makes this generator ideally suited for use with sensitive electronics (e.g., computer, fax, printer, and telephone, stereo) because the generator produces a clean sine wave of energy that prevents surging. To prevent any possible damage to the unit, the Oil Alert will shut the unit off when the oil drops below a safe operating level. The unit also has a DC terminal for use when charging 12-volt automotive type batteries. The available power is commonly used for the following typical electric devices:

  • Full Size Microwave Ovens
  • Larger Power Tools i.e. circular saw
  • Resistive Load Kitchen Appliances (i.e. Coffee Maker, Toaster, small a/c units etc).

    Two like EU series generators can be linked in parallel to increase available wattage through the generator outlets.

Second, the salesman said two generators would handle running the a/c and the other appliances at the same time. They do not! We tested them twice. This was our very first RV purchase, as we told the salesman, so we relied on his "expertise" because we had no clue.... That was our first mistake. Recommendation: Do your homework! We understand that ratings for generators are always on the high side, to allow for extra start-up draws of electrical appliances, which sometimes can be double the appliance's ratings, until they settle down. I have had no problem running a large 1500 watt microwave oven with start up at 2200 watts or more. Even running our full size refrigerator (new) and freezer (old) at the same time during power outages is no problem for my 2000. One has to add up the wattage of the appliances they are using and then add about 25% to allow for start up draw, Turning on appliances one at a time after the gen has warmed up helps too. Two EU2000 Gens are going to give you about 3000 continuous use watts....not 4000. It's a numbers game that all genset companies play. If one truly need 4000 watts, one should get a 6000 watt gen so it isn't running full speed all the time. If one uses their genset within these guidelines, no matter what brand, it should perform well for years. I prefer the honda because of the weight to noise to power ratios. A two cycle engine would be lighter and less expensive, but they are inherently more noisy and smokey because of adding oil to the fuel. Over all, Honda's engineering is second to none going back to the early 60s, when they first introduced the 50cc motorbike to the USA, many of which are still running. If my Honda genset ever wears out, I will definitely get another Honda.

    Does the EU2000i require an external ground?
    1 answer(s)
    by MarciB May 24, 2015



    For the most part, no. NEC Article 250.34 "Generators-Portable and Vehicle-Mounted" have an exemption regarding this as well as OSHA 29 CFR 1926.404(f)(3)(i). Honda manufactures their generators with a ground terminal that is "connected to the frame of the generator, the metal non-current-carrying parts of the generator, and the ground terminals of each receptacle."

    Before the use of an external ground is configured, consultation with local jurisdiction will be required as there are subset permissions/regulations on proper grounding.
